Types of Loans

1. Personal Loan

Description: An unsecured loan for personal use, often used for debt consolidation, home improvements, or major purchases.

Requirements:

  • Must be at least 18 years old.
  • Stable income and employment history.
  • Minimum credit score requirement (typically around 580).
  • Proof of identity (e.g., government-issued ID).
  • Proof of residence (e.g., utility bill or lease agreement).

2. Home Loan (Mortgage)

Description: A loan specifically for purchasing real estate, secured by the property itself.

Requirements:

  • Stable income and employment history.
  • Minimum credit score (typically 620 or higher).
  • Down payment (usually between 3% and 20% of the property value).
  • Proof of income (e.g., pay stubs, tax returns).
  • Debt-to-income ratio (DTI) must be within acceptable limits.
  • Property appraisal and insurance.

3. Auto Loan

Description: A secured loan to finance the purchase of a vehicle.

Requirements:

  • Valid driver's license.
  • Stable income and employment history.
  • Minimum credit score requirement (typically around 600).
  • Proof of insurance for the vehicle.
  • Down payment (may vary based on lender and vehicle cost).

4. Student Loan

Description: A loan designed to help students pay for education-related expenses, including tuition, books, and living costs.

Requirements:

  • Must be enrolled at an accredited educational institution.
  • Completion of the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) for federal loans.
  • For private loans, proof of income or a co-signer may be required.
  • Minimum credit score (for private loans; federal loans may not have this requirement).

5. Business Loan

Description: A loan intended for business purposes, which can be used for start-up costs, expansion, or operating expenses.

Requirements:

  • Business plan outlining the purpose of the loan.
  • Minimum credit score (varies by lender, typically 650+ for better rates).
  • Proof of business revenue (tax returns, financial statements).
  • Collateral may be required, depending on the loan amount.
  • Legal documents (e.g., business registration, licenses).

6. Payday Loan

Description: A short-term, high-cost loan typically due on the borrower's next payday.

Requirements:

  • Must be at least 18 years old.
  • Proof of income (pay stubs or bank statements).
  • A checking account is typically required.
  • Identification (e.g., government-issued ID).
  • Some lenders may require a credit check.

7. Home Equity Loan / Line of Credit (HELOC)

Description: A loan that allows homeowners to borrow against the equity in their home.

Requirements:

  • Must have sufficient equity in the home (usually 15-20% or more).
  • Stable income and employment history.
  • Minimum credit score requirement (typically around 620).
  • Proof of residence and homeowner's insurance.
  • Appraisal of the home may be required.
